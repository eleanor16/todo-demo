Why this page exists

This semester I'm taking a class to learn the basics of web design. Now, I know what you might be thinking-what does web design have to do with chemical engineering?? Trust me, I've thought about it too, but this is 2018, and I've been wanting to learn a computer language other than MATLAB for some time now, so here I am. Now you might be thinking, "Hannah, what do you hope to do by learning things like HTML, CSS, and Javascript?" Well, I'm so glad you asked! (If you didn't, now's your chance to). You see, in addition to my nerdy classes, I'm also in charge of publicity for two student organizations on my campus. Because of these positions, I have to manage and update our websites pretty often. However, the website for one of these groups (AIChE-IIT) doesn't look very pretty or professional, and the other group doesn't even have a website right now. I'm hoping to be able to make a site for one of these clubs during this semester and then be able to make another one for the other organization sometime in the future (maybe during one of my holiday breaks). So there you have it. I'm trying to learn how to make webpages so that I can do my PR job better.

What I'm doing with my life right now

Alright, new friend, it's time to talk about my major again. First of all, I'm a third-year student, so I'm pretty much halfway through with college. Now, let's talk about what I've done for the past 2 years here and what I hope to do during the last two. But first, let's step back a little more and discover why I even want to be a chemical engineer in the first place. (I actually get this question a lot-usually coupled with a grimace). When I was a senior in high school, I decided that I really wanted to work for L'Oreal because of a girls in STEM campaign they ran. There was only one problem-I didn't know what major to pursue in order to get hired for their R&D department. Lucky for me, I met the dean of engineering on one a college visits in Indiana, and he told me that I should study chemical engineering if I wanted to get in the company's good graces. I took that info and ran with it, and here I am today. For the past two years I've studied for my classes and gotten really involved with my campus's ChemE club, AIChE. During my last two years I want to take a step back from some of my PR duties for them so I can focus on research and internships.

Enjoying the Journey

After I graduate, I hope to either get a job relating to pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, or nuclear energy. However, I've also recently been thinking about moving back home to get my masters in chemical or agricultural engineering. (I mean, I did grow up surrounded by corn). I'm not really sure what these next two years and life beyond this campus holds for me, but I'm learning to be content with the unknown and enjoy the journey.
